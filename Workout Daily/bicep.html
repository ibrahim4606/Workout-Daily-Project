Biceps Workout :

Here's a bodyweight bicep workout you can do at home without any equipment. It includes 10 exercises, each offering variations for different difficulty levels. Remember to modify or skip exercises as needed to maintain proper form and avoid injury.

Note :

  Take 30 - 45 Sec. break between each Workout

Workout

1.Regular Bicep Curls: ( 3x12 )

  • Stand with your feet shoulder-width apart and arms at your sides.

  • Bend your elbows, raising your hands towards your shoulders.

  • Squeeze your biceps at the top and slowly lower your hands back down.

  • Beginner :  Do curls while holding onto a sturdy piece of furniture for support.

  • Intermediate :  Increase the number of reps or hold weights (filled water bottles, books) in your hands.

  • Advanced :  Try single-arm curls or hammer curls (palms facing inward).


2.Diamond Push-ups: ( 3x12 )

  • Beginner :  Keep your hands wider than shoulder-width apart.

  • Intermediate :  Bring your hands closer together, forming a diamond shape with your thumbs and forefingers.

  • Advanced :  Do decline diamond push-ups with your feet elevated.


3.Negative Pull-ups: ( 3x12 )

  • Find a sturdy bar or pull-up bar.

  • Jump up to the top position of a pull-up.

  • Slowly lower yourself down with control as far as you can.

  • This exercise also works your back and shoulders.

  • Beginner :  Use a step or chair to assist you in reaching the top position.

  • Intermediate :  Focus on the slow and controlled lowering motion.

  • Advanced :  Try jumping higher to start or adding weight to your body.


4.Chair Tricep Dips: ( 3x12 )

  • Sit on the edge of a sturdy chair with your hands shoulder-width apart on the edge. Slide your body off the chair so your arms are straight and your feet are flat on the floor.

  • Lower your body down until your elbows are bent at a 90-degree angle.Push back up to the starting position.This exercise also works your triceps.

  • Beginner :  Bend your knees for more support.

  • Intermediate :  Keep your legs straight out in front of you.

  • Advanced :  Place your feet on a bench or chair for a greater challenge.


5.Wall Push-ups: ( 3x12 )

  • Stand facing a wall, arms shoulder-width apart, hands flat against the wall at shoulder height.

  • Lean forward, keeping your body straight and core engaged, until your chest nearly touches the wall.

  • Push back to the starting position.

  • Beginner :  Keep your feet closer to the wall for less resistance.

  • Intermediate :  Move your feet further away from the wall for more resistance.


6.Superman Hold: ( 3x30sec )

  • Lie on your stomach with your arms and legs extended out straight.

  • Lift your head, chest, arms, and legs off the ground as high as you can.

  • Squeeze your shoulder blades together and hold for 30 seconds.

  • This exercise also works your back and core.

  • Beginner :  Start with shorter holds (15-20 seconds).

  • Intermediate :  ncrease the hold time.

  • Advanced :  Try lifting one arm or leg higher than the other.


7.Isometric Bicep Curls: ( 3x12 )

  • Stand with your feet shoulder-width apart and arms bent at 90 degrees, as if holding weights. Tense your biceps muscles as hard as you can for 30 seconds.

  • Relax and repeat.

  • Beginner :  Start with shorter holds (15-20 seconds).

  • Intermediate :  Increase the hold time.

  • Advanced :  Try doing single-arm isometric curls.
