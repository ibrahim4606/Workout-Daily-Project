Back Workout :

Here's a killer back workout that you can do at home without any equipment! It includes 10 bodyweight exercises to target different muscles in your back. Remember to modify or skip exercises as needed to maintain proper form and avoid injury.

Note :

  Take 30 - 45 Sec. break between each Workout

Workout

1.Superman Hold: ( 3x30sec )

  • Lie on your stomach with your arms and legs extended out straight.

  • Lift your head, chest, arms, and legs off the ground as high as you can.

  • Squeeze your shoulder blades together and hold for 30 seconds.

  • This exercise also works your back and core.

  • Beginner :  Start with shorter holds (15-20 seconds).

  • Intermediate :  ncrease the hold time.

  • Advanced :  Try lifting one arm or leg higher than the other.


2.Swimming: ( 3x12 )

  • Lie on your stomach with your arms and legs extended out straight.

  • Simultaneously lift your right arm and left leg off the ground a few inches.

  • Hold for a second, then lower them back down.

  • Repeat with the opposite arm and leg.


3.Cat-Cows: ( 3x12 )

  • Start on your hands and knees with your hands shoulder-width apart and your knees hip-width apart.

  • As you inhale, arch your back and look up towards the ceiling (cow pose).

  • As you exhale, round your back and tuck your chin to your chest (cat pose).


4.Dead Bugs: ( 3x12 )

  • Lie on your back with your knees bent and feet flat on the floor.

  • Extend one arm straight up towards the ceiling and the opposite leg straight out in front of you.

  • Lower your arm and leg down towards the floor at the same time, but don't let your lower back touch the ground.

  • Repeat with the other arm and leg.


5.Bird Dog: ( 3x12 )

  • Start on your hands and knees with your hands shoulder-width apart and your knees hip


6.Wall Push-ups: ( 3x12 )

  • Stand facing a wall, arms shoulder-width apart, hands flat against the wall at shoulder height.

  • Lean forward, keeping your body straight and core engaged, until your chest nearly touches the wall.

  • Push back to the starting position.

  • Beginner :  Keep your feet closer to the wall for less resistance.

  • Intermediate :  Move your feet further away from the wall for more resistance.


7.Plank: ( 3x30sec )

  • Start in a high plank position with your forearms on the floor and your body in a straight line from head to heels.

  • Engage your core and hold the position for as long as you can.
