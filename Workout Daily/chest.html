Workout

1.Regular Push-ups: ( 3x12 )

  • Beginner :  Do push-ups from your knees.

  • Intermediate :  Elevate your hands on a bench, chair, or sturdy object.

  • Advanced :  Do decline push-ups with your feet elevated on a bench or chair.


2.Diamond Push-ups: ( 3x12 )

  • Beginner :  Keep your hands wider than shoulder-width apart.

  • Intermediate :  Bring your hands closer together, forming a diamond shape with your thumbs and forefingers.

  • Advanced :  Do decline diamond push-ups with your feet elevated.


3.Incline Push-ups: ( 3x12 )

  • Find a sturdy object like a bench, chair, or sofa to elevate your upper body.

  • Place your hands shoulder-width apart and lean forward, keeping your body in a straight line.

  • Beginner :  Start with a lower incline.

  • Intermediate :  Increase the incline angle as you get stronger.

  • Advanced :  Use a wall or other vertical surface for a steep incline.


4.Decline Push-ups: ( 3x12 )

  • Find a sturdy object like a bench, chair, or sofa to elevate your feet.

  • Place your hands shoulder-width apart and keep your body in a straight line.

  • Beginner :  Start with a lower decline.

  • Intermediate :  Increase the decline angle as you get stronger.

  • Advanced :  Use an exercise ball or other unstable surface for an added challenge


5.Wall Push-ups: ( 3x12 )

  • Stand facing a wall, arms shoulder-width apart, hands flat against the wall at shoulder height.

  • Lean forward, keeping your body straight and core engaged, until your chest nearly touches the wall.

  • Push back to the starting position.

  • Beginner :  Keep your feet closer to the wall for less resistance.

  • Intermediate :  Move your feet further away from the wall for more resistance.


6.Archer Push-ups: ( 3x12 )

  • Assume a push-up position with one hand slightly forward and the other hand behind your back.

  • Lower your chest towards the ground, keeping your core engaged and back straight.

  • Push back up to the starting position.

  • Repeat on the other side.

  • Beginner :  Do this exercise on your knees.

  • Intermediate :  Perform the exercise with both hands on the ground, but keep your arms wider than shoulder-width apart.

  • Advanced :  Do archer push-ups with your feet elevated on a bench or chair.


7.Knee Push-ups: ( 3x12 )

  • Start in a kneeling position with your hands shoulder-width apart on the floor.

  • Lower your chest towards the ground, keeping your back straight and core engaged.

  • Push back to the starting position.

  • Beginner :  Keep your hands closer together for more stability.

  • Intermediate :  Move your hands further apart for more challenge.


8.Plank with Arm Raises: ( 3x12 )

  • Start in a high plank position with your forearms on the floor and your body in a straight line from head to heels.

  • Lift one arm off the ground, reaching towards the ceiling.

  • Hold for a second, then lower your arm back down.

  • Repeat
